About Us

Northwest Mason Bee was assembled in 2013 with the idea of getting the community intimately involved in the protection and production in their food supply through active involvement with the PNW native Blue Orchard Mason Bee.

Currently, over 100 major U.S. crops are dependent upon honey bees for pollination, with over 80% of those bees being used exclusively for Almonds. Colony collapse disorder has made honey bees expensive, and unreliable.

Because the Blue Orchard Mason Bee is resistant to colony collapse, has a higher pollination success rate, and that a single Mason Bee does the work of over 100 honey bees, this native pollinator can be leveraged by the community with the long term goal of providing consistent and reliable pollination to our regions farmers at a better price.
